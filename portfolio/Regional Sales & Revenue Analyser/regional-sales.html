Executive Summary

This project analyses regional agricultural sales and revenue across nine South African provinces using a dataset of over 8,700 sales transactions. The goal was to identify revenue trends, top-performing products, leading suppliers, and category-level insights — all presented through an interactive Excel dashboard with slicer-driven filtering by province and year.

Problem Statement

Agricultural businesses operating across multiple provinces often lack a consolidated view of sales performance. Without regional breakdowns, it becomes difficult to identify which provinces, products, or suppliers are driving revenue - and which are underperforming.

Solution & Technical Implementation

  • SQL Querying: Wrote queries to extract revenue by province, top products, category breakdowns, supplier performance, and monthly trends.
  • Power Query Transformation: Added calculated columns for Revenue (ZAR), Province, Month, and Year from the raw dataset.
  • PivotTable Analysis: Built six PivotTables covering all key dimensions of the sales data.
  • Interactive Dashboard: Province and Year slicers connected to all six PivotCharts for dynamic filtering.
  • KPI Cards: Formula-driven cards displaying Total Revenue, Top Province, Top Product, and Top Category.

Key Impact

  • Revenue Insight: Analyzed R413 million in total revenue across 8700+ transactions, identifying Mpumalanga as top province and Lamb as highest revenue product.
  • Regional Visibility: Province-level revenue breakdown for faster, evidence-based decisions.
  • Supplier Intelligence: Top supplier per province helps procurement teams identify value drivers.
  • Inventory Optimisation: Units Sold vs On Hand highlights overstocking or understocking by category.
  • Interactive Reporting: Any stakeholder can filter by province or year without technical skills.
